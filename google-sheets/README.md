## `google-sheets` files documentation

This folder contains CSV exports from a Google Sheet, used as qualitative and strategic inputs for the US fitness supplements market analysis.

### `Supplement short analysis - Recommendations.csv`
- **Role**: Executive summary and final recommendations of the study.  
- **Main contents**:  
  - Numbered “KEY FINDINGS” with signals (Google Trends and Meta Ads data) and conclusions.  
  - Justification of why creatine is the biggest opportunity, why the “weight loss” angle is in decline, and why to prioritize UGC/creator formats.  
- **Use**: Basis for investment decisions, presentation narrative, and prioritization of categories/creative angles.  

### `Supplement short analysis - Matrix.csv`
- **Role**: Creative strategy matrix by persona.  
- **Main contents**:  
  - Three persona columns: fitness women (25–40), gym bros (18–30), and health-conscious beginners (30–50).  
  - For each: main driver, concept/angle, trigger, recommended format, hook type, hook example, platform, keyword signals, market gap, and opportunity score.  
- **Use**: Design messages and campaigns tailored to each audience archetype.  

### `Supplement short analysis - Keyword Research.csv`
- **Role**: Keyword research table for SEO/SEM.  
- **Main contents**:  
  - For each keyword: monthly search intent, competition level, trend (↑, →, ↓), seasonality, opportunity level (color icon), and notes.  
  - Includes terms like `creatine`, `creatine monohydrate`, `best creatine for women`, `protein powder`, `weight loss supplements`, `omega 3 benefits`, etc.  
- **Use**: Identify which terms to prioritize in search campaigns and organic/paid content.  

### `Supplement short analysis - Ads Analysis .csv`
- **Role**: Analysis of specific ads from competitors and reference brands.  
- **Main contents**:  
  - Per-ad columns: brand, ad ID, active since, duration (days), platform, format, audience, creative concept, hook, CTA, and signal (winner/testing).  
  - Examples: Black Girl Vitamins, Emma x GNC, NaturalSlim, REDCON1, Oats Overnight, etc.  
- **Use**: Understand which types of creatives, audiences, and messages sustain performance over time and serve as benchmarks for new campaigns.  

### `Supplement short analysis - Sentiment Analysis.csv`
- **Role**: Summary of sentiment and conversation analysis on X (Twitter).  
- **Main contents**:  
  - Findings about “creatine for women”, main fears/objections (hair loss, kidneys, bloating, “it will make me bulky”, etc.).  
  - Sentiment map by brand/ingredient (Creapure, Optimum Nutrition, Muscle Feast, etc.).  
  - Recommended copy angles derived from real conversations.  
- **Use**: Inspire ad and content messages that address objections and reinforce the strongest purchase drivers.  

### `Supplement short analysis - Trends.csv`
- **Role**: Numeric summary of trends per keyword.  
- **Main contents**:  
  - For each keyword: Feb 2025 vs Feb 2026 averages, YoY change, peak and peak month, trend (↑, →, ↓), and priority (High/Medium/Low/Avoid).  
  - Includes at least creatine, protein powder, omega 3, pre workout, and weight loss supplements.  
- **Use**: Connect Google Trends data with a priority level per category for investment decisions and strategic focus.
